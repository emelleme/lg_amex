Tomato Tartlets

Back

  • Kosher salt and freshly ground pepper
  • 2 Tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 lb fresh ricotta
  • All-purpose flour
  • 30 cherry tomatoes (about 1 lb), halved
  • 1/2 lb all-butter puff pastry
  • 2 tsp fresh thyme leaves,
    plus more for garnish

Preheat oven to 425Â° and line a large baking sheet with parchment. On a lightly floured surface, trim the puff pastry into a 9-by-17 inch rectangle. Transfer to the baking sheet and poke all over with a fork; bake for 25 mins until lightly browned and dry. Meanwhile, in a large bowl, toss tomatoes, olive oil and thyme and season with salt and pepper; bake, cut side up, on upper rack for about 15 mins, until softened slightly. Puree the ricotta until creamy and spread over the pastry. Arrange the tomatoes on the ricotta and sprinkle lightly with fresh thyme.

Crab Rolls with Lemon Aioli

Back

  • 1 1/4 cups aioli or mayonnaise
  • 2 large celery ribs, cut into 1/4-inch dice
  • 2 Tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • Cayenne pepper
  • Salt
  • 1 1/4 lbs lump crabmeat, lightly broken up
  • 8 hot dog buns or 16 mini brioche rolls
  • 8 Boston lettuce leaves, sliced

In a large bowl, mix the mayonnaise with the celery and lemon juice and season with cayenne. Gently fold in the crabmeat and season with salt. Fill the buns with the lettuce and the crab salad and serve.

Boiled Shrimp with Spicy Mayonnaise

Back

  • 2 very fresh organic egg yolks
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp hot mustard powder
  • 1 tsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 cup canola oil
  • Salt and freshly ground white pepper
  • 1 Tbsp water
  • 2 lbs medium shrimp, peeled and deveined

In a medium bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, mustard powder and lemon juice. Whisking constantly, slowly dribble in the canola oil. Season with salt and white pepper to taste. Whisk in water to thin if necessary. Bring a large saucepan of water to a boil. Add the shrimp and boil until pink, about 3 minutes. Drain and let cool. Serve with the spicy mayonnaise.

Champagne Cocktail

Back

  • 1 sugar cube
  • 3 dashes of Angostura bitters
  • 5 ounces chilled brut Champagne
  • 1 lemon twist, for garnish

In a small dish or glass, soak the sugar cube with the Angostura bitters. Fill a chilled flute with the Champagne, then add the bitters-soaked sugar cube. Garnish the drink with the lemon twist.
